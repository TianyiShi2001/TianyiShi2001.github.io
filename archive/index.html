For many years, I have been long for having a website of my own—I mean, I have the full control of the website, from the content to the styling and to the functions—not based on any prebuit templates.

I had't touched HTML, CSS and JS (not to mention backend languages) until 0th week of Trinity term of Year 1, so building up this website was quite crazy—I compromised a significant amount of time supposed to be used for prelim revision.

But I made it, though this website is still largely incomplete. Beside a tutorial essay, there's a gallery of my photographs (also largely incomplete), and you can access it though the nav bar (content --> gallery)

In the Easter vacation, I intended to write a book about biophysical chemistry focused on derivations of equations and tips for solving problems (beutifully typeset with LaTeX!). Unfortunately I did not have enough time, energy and motivation to finish it. Only the chapter "enzyme kinetics" was finished, and the book can be found here.

I also made a scorekeeper for Doudizhu (a Chinese card game) using javascript.
